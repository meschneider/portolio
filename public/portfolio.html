    Project Overview During the spring of 2017 I worked as a Data Analyst with two other students to analyze data for social good. We chose to analyze population and traffic stop data of San Francisco, California to determine if race correlated with the proportion of traffic stops and the outcome of the stops. For this project, I cleaned our data which combined had over 100,000 entries. Once our data was cleaned, I created graphs, performed chi-squared analysis, and determined the effect size for our data. This project expanded my understanding of data analysis and allowed me to learn a new programing language, Python. Specifics DURATION: April 2017– June 2017 ROLE: Data Analyst APPLICATIONS: Python METHODS: Data Cleaning, Chi-Squared Analysis, Effect Size
